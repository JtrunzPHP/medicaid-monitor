Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) Rate Study Background

The State of Hawai`i Med-QUEST Division (MQD) conducted an Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) rate study for its Medicaid program. This work is in compliance with the Hawai`i State Legislature’s House Resolution #207 passed in 2023.The project provides insight into the current costs associated with ABA service delivery and staffing requirements. 

The 2023 ABA Rate Study includes the following ABA Services:

  • Behavior Identification Assessment
  • Behavior Identification Supporting Assessment
  • Direct Treatment by Protocol
  • Group Treatment by Protocol
  • Direct Treatment with Protocol Modification
  • Family Training
  • Multiple-Family Training
  • Group Treatment with Protocol Modification
  • Direct Treatment of Severe Maladaptive Behavior

The rate study includes­­­ a range for “comparison” payment rates for MQD and legislature’s consideration. Any funding increases will need to be appropriated by the state legislature to be implemented. If approved, final comparison rates will be published for the use of providers and MCOs to consider when negotiating managed care contracts, and for MQD and other stakeholders to use when evaluating changes to overall funding.

HCBS Rate Study Background

The 2022 Phase I HCBS Rate Study focused on the following highly utilized services included in the MQD American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 (ARPA) spending plan:

  • Case Management Agency
  • Community Care Foster Family Home
  • Expanded Adult Residential Care Home
  • Homemaker/Companion/Chore (PA1)
  • Personal Care/Personal Assistance/Attendant Care (PA2)
  • Private Duty Nursing (RN and LPN)
  • Self-Directed Personal Assistance

The 2023 Phase II Hawai’i HCBS Rate Study built upon the work completed under Phase I and included the following services:

  • Adult Day Care
  • Adult Day Health
  • Assisted Living Facilities
  • Home Delivered Meals
